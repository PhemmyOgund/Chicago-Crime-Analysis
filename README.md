📊 Chicago Crime Data Analysis: Assault Patterns & Environmental Factors
🧠 Executive Summary

This project analyzes Chicago crime data to evaluate patterns in assault incidents and assess whether environmental factors—specifically temperature—have a measurable impact on crime levels. Using advanced Excel techniques, the analysis combines data transformation, statistical exploration, and visualization to derive actionable insights.

🎯 Business Problem

Understanding the drivers of crime is critical for:

Law enforcement resource allocation
Public safety planning
Policy decision-making

This analysis investigates whether temperature influences assault rates, a commonly assumed relationship, and identifies temporal patterns across days of the week.

🛠️ Tools & Techniques
Microsoft Excel
Pivot Tables for aggregation
VLOOKUP for data integration
Data Cleaning & Structuring
Data Visualization
Bar Charts
Scatter Plots
Histograms
Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)
🔄 Data Preparation
Consolidated datasets from multiple worksheets using VLOOKUP
Validated and aligned key variables (date, temperature, assault counts)
Structured data for analysis using consistent formats
📈 Analytical Approach
🔹 1. Aggregation & Trend Analysis
Used Pivot Tables to compute:
Average assault incidents by day of the week
Average temperature by day of the week
🔹 2. Relationship Analysis
Built a scatter plot to evaluate correlation between:
Assault incidents
Average temperature
🔹 3. Distribution Analysis
Created histograms to examine:
Distribution of assault incidents
Distribution of temperature
Assessed whether distributions are:
Symmetric (normal)
Skewed (biased toward high or low values)
🔍 Key Insights
📌 1. No Correlation Between Temperature and Assault
The scatter plot analysis revealed no significant relationship between temperature and assault incidents
This challenges the assumption that higher temperatures directly increase violent crime
📌 2. Symmetric Distribution of Data
Both temperature and assault data showed approximately symmetric distributions
Indicates:
No extreme skewness
Balanced spread of observations
📌 3. Day-of-Week Patterns
Assault incidents vary across different days of the week
Suggests that temporal factors (behavioral or social patterns) may be more influential than environmental conditions
💡 Business Implications
Resource allocation should prioritize time-based patterns rather than temperature assumptions
Law enforcement strategies may benefit from focusing on high-risk days
Data-driven approaches are essential to challenge assumptions and guide decision-making
